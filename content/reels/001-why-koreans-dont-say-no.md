
# Reel 001

## Title

Why don't Koreans say "No"?

---

## Hook (0~3s)

Have you noticed?

Koreans almost never say "No."

---

## Curiosity

Instead of saying "No,"

they often say

"Maybe..."

"I'll think about it."

or

"It might be difficult."

Why?

---

## Korean OS

In Korean culture,

maintaining harmony is often more important than giving a direct answer.

The goal is not to avoid honesty,

but to protect the relationship.

---

## Example

A:
Can you come tomorrow?

B:
It might be difficult...

↓

Meaning:

Probably no.

---

## Takeaway

Korean is not indirect.

It is relationship-oriented.
